To Be a Doctor

What is a Doctor?

A Doctor is a human being who maintains his role of "God" and maintains or restores human health through the practice of medicine and medical treatment. He or she will diagnose and treat human illnesses, ailments, injuries, pain or other conditions. A doctor can be found in many settings, including public health organizations, hospitals, educational institutions, private practices, group practices.


What does a Doctor Do?

A doctor usually works 24 hours a day and has long duties at all times and must be available for emergencies. These hours are spent seeing patients in an office, performing and interpreting tests, prescribing medications or treatments, making hospital rounds, taking notes on the physical condition of patients. He, counsel and talk to patients about how to stay healthy. They keep up to date by taking courses and regularly reading books and medical journals. A doctor who also performs surgeries typically works two or three full days in the office, then two or three days in the hospital operating room performing surgeries. Physicians will also devote time to performing administrative tasks such as updating patient records, returning phone calls, or dealing with various office issues.


How to become a doctor and what are the ways for being a Doctor after 12th:

  1. Eligibility criteria for appearing for pre-medical test i.e. NEET-UG is securing > 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry and Biology/ Biotechnology.
    2. If you meet these eligibility criteria, then the next step would be to appear for NEET-UG.
  2. Decide whether you want to appear for MBBS (Medical stream) or BDS (Dental stream) and then appear for the NEET UG exam accordingly.
  3. Results of NEET-UG will be in the form of an All India Rank and State rank (of which you hold the domicile)
  4. All India Quota is 15% MBBS/BDS seats of any college, while the remaining 85% seats belong to the respective state under whose administration falls the college.
  5. Once you secure the rank, take part in the counselling (either All India or State).
  6. Once you're allotted a college, take admission there into the course that you had initially opted for i.e. MBBS or BDS.
  7. After completion of your course (MBBS/BDS) you have to undergo a 1 year training period a.k.a. Internship. Once you complete.
  8. After completion of your graduation i.e. MBBS/BDS, you need to get registered in either MCI or State medical council. And then you officially can write ‘Dr.’ as prefix before your name.
  9. After MBBS/BDS you will have to provide compulsory rural service, implementation and duration of which depends from state to state.
  10. Post rural service, you can either enter formal practice, OR you can prepare for Post graduate entrance examination known as NEET-PG (for Medical) or NEET-MDS (for Dental).
